The actual problem

It was never about forgetting to write it down.

You wrote it down. You said "I'll send you their contact information later." You meant every word of it. The problem is what happens after that — the part every other app ignores.

Cognitive Overload

Your phone is already good at saving things: a link, a voicemail, a note to self; those things are just fragmented, leading to an overwhelming workflow that causes depletion of your limited time and energy.

Notification Fatigue and Overlook

A banner competes with everything else on your screen, and loses. One swipe and it's gone, like it never fired at all. Standard app notifications are easily ignored or forgotten; they don't account for any executive function barriers.

Heading

The thing that actually needs solving isn't "where do I put this." It's "what makes sure I don't quietly let it go."

Why it's built this way

Task management,
tailored for you

No two brains that struggle with follow-through struggle the same way. Most software asks you to learn its system. Later Gator is meant to be personalized.

Time management AND energy management

Most task apps front-load configuration. They ask you to think, plan, and organize with every task or nothing happens. Later Gator inverts that. Capture first, configure only if you want to, but still be useful.

Organization is a means to an end

The thing that actually needs solving isn't finding a perfect organizational system, but making sure you actually complete the follow-up.
