Now — in the prototype

What's actually built and working in the live prototype you're looking at right now.

Capture & organization

Send anything from any app straight into Later Gator in two taps.

The OS share sheet is the front door. A link, voicemail, photo, or text thread becomes a task without opening Later Gator first or typing anything — the same motion you already use to send something to a friend.

Color-coded view of every task, sorted by how long it's been waiting.

Every task shifts color the longer it sits untouched, from cool blue to charcoal. You never have to read a list top to bottom to know what's actually burning — the color tells you before the words do.

Reminders

Set a timeframe or deadline, then pick how and how often you're reminded.

Choose "today," "this week," or a specific date and time, then layer on channel (push, eventually email/SMS), frequency, and how intense the alert should feel — from a quiet banner to something that won't let go.

Reflection

Quick mood, energy, and difficulty check before and after a task.

A fast, skippable set of questions when you start and finish something — how it felt, how hard it actually was, how long it really took. Over time this builds a real picture of your patterns, not a guess.

Next — early access

What's being built right now, ahead of the first real install outside this prototype.

Alerts

Banner → sound → persistent alert, actually firing on your phone instead of just in the demo.

The home page demo shows what this looks like sped up. This is that same ladder, live: if a banner gets swiped away, the next step gets louder, automatically.

Capture & organization

Defer configuring a task without losing it — get nudged to finish later instead of abandoning it.

Most apps treat an incomplete task as your problem to remember. Later Gator treats unfinished setup as its own remindable event, so half-filled-out tasks don't just vanish because you got interrupted.

A short set of questions about how you check tasks and what kind of nudge actually works on you.

Instead of a generic tutorial, a handful of questions about your habits and preferences set your starting reminder style, focus session length, and notification intensity automatically — used to set defaults, not just collected and ignored.

Cognitive load tools

Estimate how much capacity a task takes and how much you're dreading it, then plan around your actual state.

Two new attributes per task: Energy (how much mental or physical capacity it'll take) and Dread (how much you're avoiding it). On a low-energy day, you can see what's actually realistic instead of guessing.

More honest than done/not done: Not Started, In Progress, Partially Complete, or Complete.

Real progress isn't binary. This lets a task reflect that you started it, got interrupted, and are most of the way there — instead of forcing an all-or-nothing checkbox.

Platform

First real install for early testers, outside of this prototype.

The actual app, not a demo — installed on your phone through Apple's TestFlight program, ahead of any public App Store release.

Later — the bigger vision

Where this could go with the right team and validated demand. Not promises — a real, honest picture of the vision.

Alerts

Lights, speakers, and flashlight strobe as the last, hardest-to-ignore step.

If a banner, sound, and persistent alert haven't worked, the reminder can move into your physical space — a smart bulb changing color, a speaker announcing the task, or your own phone's flashlight strobing until you deal with it.

Get reminded by where you are or who just reached out, not just by the clock.

A task tied to "the hardware store" can remind you when you're actually near one. A task waiting on a reply from someone specific can resurface the moment they text.

Different snooze lengths each time, multiple alert types per task, and alarms from your own music library.

Escalation doesn't have to be identical every time — the first snooze can be short, the next longer, and the sound itself can be something you actually chose.

Cognitive load tools

Chain tasks together, or group several into a routine where finishing one nudges you about the rest.

"Pack the bag" can wait until "do laundry" is done. A morning routine can group five small tasks so completing one prompts the next, instead of tracking each separately.

Let AI break an intimidating task into smaller, startable pieces.

A vague, heavy task like "deal with insurance" can be split into a few concrete first steps automatically — the kind of breakdown that's hard to do for yourself when the task itself feels like too much.

A quick, judgment-free check-in when you delete a task, not just when you finish one.

Most apps only ask how it went when you succeed. This asks what got in the way when you don't — building a real record of what kinds of tasks tend to get dropped, and why.

Accessibility

Built in, not bolted on.

Full voice command support, tasks and alerts read aloud, and a high-contrast display mode — designed in from the start rather than retrofitted as an afterthought.

Collaboration

Hand a task to someone else, or drop into a distraction-free timer when you're ready to actually do the thing.

Share a task or list with one person or a group. Separately, a Pomodoro-style focus timer with distraction blocking for when you've decided right now is the moment.

Some groups split the work. Others just keep you honest.

One group type shares ownership of tasks and lists directly. The other is accountability-only — someone checks in on your progress without taking on any of the task themselves.

For coaches, employers, and schools supporting someone else's follow-through.

Built for the cases where the person paying isn't the person using the app — a parent, a coach, an employer covering licenses for a team that needs the extra structure.

Gamification

A lightweight game layer — treasure, badges, and group challenges — designed to support task completion, not distract from it.

You're cast as a pirate; the gator is the thing that catches up to you when something's overdue. Completing tasks earns treasure and badges, with optional group challenges for anyone who finds a little competition motivating.

Data

See your own patterns — what kinds of tasks you stall on, when you're most likely to follow through.

A private dashboard of your own completion times, snooze patterns, and energy trends over time, so you can see what's actually true about how you work instead of guessing.

Form factor

A distraction-free, always-visible version of your task bank for a wall or counter.

For anyone who wants Later Gator visible in the home without a phone's distractions attached to it — a dedicated tablet showing just your tasks, nothing else.
