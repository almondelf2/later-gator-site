The actual problem

It was never about forgetting to write it down.

You wrote it down. You said "send me her name." You meant every word of it. The problem is what happens after that — the part every other app ignores.

Capture isn't the gap

You're already good at saving things — a link, a voicemail, a note to self. The graveyard isn't empty. It's just a graveyard.

Reminders get swiped

A banner competes with everything else on your lock screen, and loses. One swipe and it's gone, like it never fired at all.

Follow-through is the job

The thing that actually needs solving isn't "where do I put this." It's "what makes sure I don't quietly let it go."

"She doesn't need a reminder. She needs something that won't take no for an answer."

My wife has ADHD. She's tried every app. None of them worked — not because she wasn't trying, but because her phone is a portal to distraction, not a tool for focus. Later Gator doesn't ask her to change. It meets her where she already is — a planner she loves, a sticky-note system that works for about a week, and a closet of abandoned apps that all assumed she'd build a habit around them instead of the other way around.

The design philosophy

Capture first. Configure only if you want to.

Most task apps front-load the thinking — they ask you to plan before you've even committed to writing something down. Later Gator inverts that.

01
Zero configuration

Just capture it

Drop it in, Later Gator handles the rest. Heat-based escalation runs automatically from the moment something lands — no due date, no setup, no decisions required. This is the default for anyone who doesn't want to think about the system at all, they just want the thing remembered.

02
Minimal configuration

Give it a nudge

Want a little more control? Set a rough urgency — this week, today, someday — and the escalation timeline adjusts without forcing you to pick an exact date or time.

03
Full configuration

Set a real deadline

For the things that actually have a due date, pin it to a specific time. Now the countdown runs toward that moment instead of just counting up from when you captured it.

It also means the app meets you at your energy level. Low energy? Just share it. High energy? Dial it in.

Why it's built this way

The app adapts to you. Not the other way around.

No two brains that struggle with follow-through struggle the same way. Most software asks you to learn its system. Later Gator is built to learn yours.

Meet, don't fix

The goal was never to make someone more organized. It's to make sure the things they already intended to do don't quietly disappear.

Progressive disclosure

Simple by default, powerful by choice. Under the hood it's a deeply configurable rules engine — but nobody has to see that unless they go looking for it.

Physical, not just digital

A notification you can swipe away isn't a consequence. The goal is to make a digital commitment feel as real as a note stuck to the fridge.
