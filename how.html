How it works

From "I'll deal with it later" to actually dealt with.

Four moments. No new habits to learn — just one extra tap, and a system that doesn't let go.

01

Share it the way you already share things

Open the share sheet on whatever you're already looking at — a text, a voicemail, a saved link — and tap Later Gator. That's the whole capture step. No typing, no app-switching, no setup required to get started.

02

Pick your level of detail — or skip this entirely

This is the three-layer model in action: leave it alone and Later Gator just tracks it from the moment it landed. Give it a rough nudge — today, this week, someday — and the timeline adjusts. Or set an exact deadline if there's a real date attached. None of these are required to move forward.

03

It waits. Then it escalates.

Nothing happens until the moment you set. After that, Later Gator follows the path you've configured — banner first, then louder, then physical if you've connected a light or speaker. You choose how far it's allowed to go.

04

Mark it done. Get a quick check-in.

When you complete a task, Later Gator asks a couple of fast questions — how it felt, how long it actually took. Skip it if you're not in the mood. Answer it and you start building a real picture of what works for you.

Color tells the story

Open the app, know instantly what's burning.

Every task shifts color the longer it sits untouched — cool blue when it's fresh, charcoal when it's been ignored too long. You never have to read a list to know what needs you first.
